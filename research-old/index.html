Research



I am currently in Prof. Rina Dechter’s group. Graphical models are a widely used framework for reasoning tasks. I am primarily focused on methods to exploit specific structures in problems such as determinism and context-specific independence. One can reduce time and memory usage substantially by taking advantage of these. I am currently exploring the AND/OR Decision Diagram framework, which generates a compressed search space for a given problem, representing the full joint function that a graphical model represents. This can also uncover the true difficulty of a problem, which common graph parameters may not be able to capture.



Before UCI, I worked as an undergraduate research assistant in R-LAIR (Riverside Lab for Artificial Intelligence Research) at UC Riverside with Prof. Christian Shelton.

I implemented the structure learning component of CTBN-RLE to learn a Bayesian network and continuous time Bayesian network structure from trajectory data. I also implemented mean field variational approximate inference for CTBNs, the algorithm described in Cohn et al. 2009. (to appear in an updated release of CTBN-RLE).
